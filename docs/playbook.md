# RackIQ Sales Playbook

## Morning routine — work the day in six moves

1. **Set the regime.** Open the Daily Operating Dashboard. Set today's inventory, market, capacity, and credit regime. Everything re-ranks instantly to match the day you're actually in.
2. **Read Today's Actions.** The top panel is your stack-ranked worklist — the highest-impact move per account. Work it top-down; each row tells you the action, the why-now, and the expected impact.
3. **Clear the alerts.** Scan Credit Alerts and Churn Alerts. Gate or shorten terms on credit risks before you allocate; call the fading/overdue accounts before they're gone.
4. **Place or protect inventory.** Inventory Actions tells you whether today is a 'move length' day or a 'protect supply' day. Work Pricing Opportunities alongside it — raise where they pay, quote thin to fill.
5. **Advance the franchise.** Before lunch, touch one Strategic Account, one Contract Candidate, and one Discount Opportunity. These are the plays that compound — don't let the day's noise crowd them out.
6. **Spot-check a scorecard.** Pull up one account's scorecard. Confirm the recommended action and read the flip-side line so you know how your plan changes if the regime turns.

## Archetype plays

For each archetype: what to say, when to call, what to quote, what terms to require, and what *not* to do.

### Anchor Base-Load

- **What to say:** “You're our priority supply — let's lock a program that rewards how steady you are.”
- **When to call:** Quarterly business review cadence; immediately if a lift slips its usual window.
- **What to quote:** Contract/program pricing with a thin loyalty spread; never the spot-of-the-day.
- **What terms to require:** Standard terms — they've earned them. Reward reliability, don't re-underwrite.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't shop them on price or leave them unallocated in a tight market — they are your floor.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Defend with contract pricing; small loyalty spread. Terms — Standard terms; reward reliability. Allocation — Protect supply first — this is your floor.

### Flex Buyer

- **What to say:** “When you're ready to move, we'll have a sharp number — tell us your window.”
- **When to call:** When inventory is long or the market is falling and you need to place volume.
- **What to quote:** Dynamic to rack; capture upside when they lean in, stay flexible.
- **What terms to require:** Standard terms.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't build your base plan around them — their timing is erratic by nature.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Dynamic to rack; capture upside when they lean in. Terms — Standard terms. Allocation — Serve after anchors; flexible.

### Premium Spot

- **What to say:** “We can guarantee the gallons today — here's the number for certainty.”
- **When to call:** When supply is tight or the market is rising/volatile — that's when they pay.
- **What to quote:** Hold the premium. They buy availability, not price; discounting leaves money on the table.
- **What terms to require:** Prepay / tight terms are acceptable and appropriate.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't discount to 'win' the deal — you'll train a premium buyer to haggle.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Hold premium — they pay for availability, not price. Terms — Prepay / tight terms acceptable. Allocation — Fill from surplus at a premium.

### Price Shopper

- **What to say:** “If we get long, you'll be the first call with a sharp number.”
- **When to call:** Only to fill troughs — long inventory or a falling market.
- **What to quote:** Thin, opportunistic numbers only; never chase, never your scarce gallons.
- **What terms to require:** Prepay; minimize credit.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't allocate tight supply or premium slots to them — lowest priority.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Quote thin only to fill troughs; never chase. Terms — Prepay; minimize credit. Allocation — Lowest priority; surplus only.

### Surplus Absorber

- **What to say:** “We've got length to move — can you take an extra parcel at a clearing price?”
- **When to call:** The moment inventory runs long or you're tank-constrained.
- **What to quote:** Clearing price for length — this protects working capital and frees tankage.
- **What terms to require:** Prepay preferred.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't sell them scarce gallons in a tight market — that's value destroyed.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Clearing price for length; protects working capital. Terms — Prepay preferred. Allocation — Use to drain long inventory.

### Scarcity Buyer

- **What to say:** “Supply's tight — we can secure your gallons, here's the number.”
- **When to call:** When the market is rising or supply is tight; they buy on availability.
- **What to quote:** Premium in tight markets; monetize the scarcity.
- **What terms to require:** Tighter terms when you're short.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't dump length on them cheap — they'll pay up when it's scarce.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Premium in tight markets; they buy on availability. Terms — Tighter terms when short. Allocation — Ration in scarcity; monetize.

### Weather-Triggered

- **What to say:** “Cold snap coming — want to pre-build before the rush and the price?”
- **When to call:** Ahead of HDD spikes; pre-season hedge conversations.
- **What to quote:** Pre-season hedge offers; premium during cold snaps.
- **What terms to require:** Seasonal credit watch — exposure swells in winter.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't assume summer cadence continues into winter — pre-build capacity.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Pre-season hedge offers; premium in cold snaps. Terms — Seasonal credit watch. Allocation — Pre-build for HDD spikes.

### Credit Drag

- **What to say:** “Let's set you up on prepay so supply is never the question.”
- **When to call:** Before allocating any volume; immediately when credit tightens.
- **What to quote:** Price in the carry; no discounts to a credit risk.
- **What terms to require:** Shorten terms / prepay; cap exposure hard.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't gate on volume — gate on credit. Don't extend more rope.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Price in the carry; no discounts. Terms — Shorten terms / prepay; cap exposure. Allocation — Gate on credit, not volume.

### Operationally Expensive

- **What to say:** “We can sharpen your price if we consolidate into fewer, larger loads.”
- **When to call:** When rack capacity is constrained; during route/schedule planning.
- **What to quote:** Add a small-order / handling surcharge; reward consolidation.
- **What terms to require:** Standard; tie better pricing to fewer, larger orders.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't let small rush orders clog the rack when slots are scarce.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Add a small-order / handling surcharge. Terms — Standard; consolidate orders. Allocation — Encourage fewer, larger loads.

### Strategic Platform

- **What to say:** “Where are you growing next? We want to grow the program with you.”
- **When to call:** Proactively and often — these are expansion conversations.
- **What to quote:** Invest spread for growth / cross-sell; play the long game.
- **What terms to require:** Flexible to deepen the relationship.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't nickel-and-dime — you're buying a franchise, not a load.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Invest spread for growth / cross-sell. Terms — Flexible to deepen the relationship. Allocation — Prioritize; expansion potential.

### Backup-Only

- **What to say:** “We're your fallback — when your primary's short, we're one call away.”
- **When to call:** When you're long and need an outlet; otherwise let them come to you.
- **What to quote:** Spot premium; you are their fallback, price accordingly.
- **What terms to require:** Prepay.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't commit supply or build forecasts on them — surplus only.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Spot premium; you are their fallback. Terms — Prepay. Allocation — Surplus only; no commitment.

### Contract Candidate

- **What to say:** “You're steady enough to earn program pricing — let's lock it in.”
- **When to call:** Now — especially before a tight market makes the volume harder to hold.
- **What to quote:** Offer a term deal to lock steady volume in exchange for a small spread.
- **What terms to require:** Contract terms; volume commitment in exchange for priority.
- **What NOT to do:** Don't leave them on spot — a competitor's contract offer can take them.
- **Standing posture:** pricing — Offer a term deal to lock steady volume. Terms — Contract terms; volume commitment. Allocation — Reserve committed volume.

## Regime cheat-sheets

When the regime selector flips, so does the plan. Read these as "if X → do Y".

### Inventory

- **Long** — _Too much product — move volume._
  - Do: Call Surplus Absorbers, Price Shoppers, Flex & Backup buyers to place length at a clearing price.
  - Don't: Don't hold out for premium — carrying length costs you daily.
- **Balanced** — _Healthy book — steady state._
  - Do: Service your anchors, keep the base humming, work contract candidates.
  - Don't: Don't chase marginal spot volume that adds friction.
- **Tight** — _Short supply — ration & monetize._
  - Do: Ration to anchors and Premium/Scarcity buyers; monetize availability.
  - Don't: Don't burn scarce gallons on price shoppers or backup-only accounts.
- **Tank-constrained** — _No room — drain length fast._
  - Do: Drain length fast — biggest, cleanest outlets first.
  - Don't: Don't take small rush orders that don't move the needle.

### Market

- **Rising** — _Prices climbing — sell forward, hold premium._
  - Do: Hold premiums, offer to lock forward, lean on Scarcity & Weather buyers.
  - Don't: Don't discount into a rising tape — you're giving away the move.
- **Falling** — _Prices dropping — move volume now._
  - Do: Move volume now; thin quotes to Price Shoppers & Flex buyers.
  - Don't: Don't sit on length waiting for a bounce that may not come.
- **Flat** — _Quiet tape — business as usual._
  - Do: Run the standard book; work the relationship and contract plays.
  - Don't: Don't manufacture urgency that isn't there.
- **Volatile** — _Whippy tape — reward stability, lock terms._
  - Do: Reward stability — lock contract candidates and anchors, hold premium for certainty.
  - Don't: Don't over-commit to one direction; keep optionality.

### Rack capacity

- **Ample** — _Plenty of rack/truck slots._
  - Do: Take the volume — even smaller loads are fine.
  - Don't: Don't add surcharges that scare off easy volume.
- **Normal** — _Typical throughput._
  - Do: Standard scheduling.
- **Constrained** — _Slots scarce — favor big, clean loads._
  - Do: Favor big, clean loads; consolidate Operationally-Expensive accounts.
  - Don't: Don't let small rush orders eat scarce rack slots.

### Credit

- **Easy** — _Credit is cheap & available._
  - Do: Compete on terms where it wins steady volume.
  - Don't: Don't over-extend just because credit is cheap today.
- **Normal** — _Standard credit posture._
  - Do: Standard credit posture.
- **Tight** — _Protect capital — gate risky accounts._
  - Do: Gate Credit-Drag accounts to prepay; protect capital before chasing gallons.
  - Don't: Don't extend terms to win marginal volume.
